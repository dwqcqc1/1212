VA Aid and Attendance for Surviving Spouses: Eligibility and Benefits

Introduction

VA Aid and Attendance is a vital benefit designed to support surviving spouses of deceased veterans by providing additional financial assistance for healthcare and daily living needs. Understanding eligibility requirements and the application process can make a significant difference in accessing this important benefit.

In this blog, we explain what VA Aid and Attendance is, who qualifies, and how to navigate the benefits and challenges involved.

Burgos & Brein Wealth Management are specialists in assisting veterans and surviving spouses with their financial and healthcare planning needs. If you have questions or need help with your application, please contact us for guidance.

What is VA Aid and Attendance?

VA Aid and Attendance (A&A) is a monthly benefit paid in addition to the basic VA Survivors Pension. It provides supplemental income to surviving spouses who need assistance with activities of daily living, such as bathing, dressing, or eating, or who are housebound due to disability.

Who is Eligible for VA Aid and Attendance?

Eligibility for Aid and Attendance is based on a combination of military service, marital status, medical needs, and financial requirements.

Military Service Requirements for the Veteran

  • The deceased veteran must have served at least 90 days of active duty, with at least one day during a wartime period as defined by the VA.
  • Service must have ended with an honorable or general discharge.

Marital Status and Length of Marriage

  • The surviving spouse must have been married to the veteran at the time of their death and must not have remarried (with some exceptions if remarriage ends in divorce or death).
  • Marriage must have lasted at least one year or produced children.

Medical and Care Needs

  • Inability to perform activities of daily living (ADLs). These include bathing, dressing, toileting, eating, or transferring (moving from bed to chair, etc.).
  • Being bedridden, residing in a nursing home, or suffering from severe visual impairment also qualifies.

Income and Asset Limits

  • Surviving spouses must meet strict income and net worth limits set by the VA, based on annual income and countable assets. Medical expenses can be deducted from countable income, increasing eligibility.

How Much Can You Receive Through Aid and Attendance?

The maximum annual benefit through Aid and Attendance depends on your income and medical expenses. For 2024, a surviving spouse may receive up to $1,433 per month (amount may be adjusted annually). The VA calculates your benefit by subtracting your countable income from the maximum pension rate.

Breakdown of Benefits:

  • Basic Survivors Pension: The base pension for surviving spouses.
  • Aid and Attendance: An extra monthly payment for those who qualify due to medical need.

For example, a surviving spouse with significant health costs but low income can receive the maximum allowable benefit. Consult the VA or a qualified advisor for your specific case.

How to Apply for VA Aid and Attendance

The process involves several important steps: gather your records, fill out forms, and prepare supporting documentation.

Gather Necessary Documents

  • Military service records (DD-214 or other service verification).
  • Marriage certificate, spouse’s death certificate, and proof of medical need (doctor's statement or care facility records).
  • Financial information, including all sources of income and asset statements.

Complete VA Forms

The main form for surviving spouses is VA Form 21P-534EZ. There is a separate form for reporting medical expenses. You can download forms from the VA website or request them by mail.

Submit the Application

Submit your completed forms and supporting documents to your local VA Pension Management Center. Keep copies of everything for your records.

Await a Decision

The VA will review your application and may request additional information or documentation. Processing times vary, so be patient, but follow up if you haven’t received a response after several weeks.

Common Challenges and How to Overcome Them

Incomplete Documentation

One of the most frequent reasons for delays or denials is missing paperwork. Make sure all forms are complete and all supporting documents included.

Complex Financial Requirements

Accurately reporting income and medical costs is essential. Don’t hesitate to seek help from an accredited VA advisor, elder law attorney, or financial planner to ensure your application is filled out correctly.

Denial of Benefits

If your application is denied, you have the right to appeal the decision. You’ll receive details on why you were denied and how to submit an appeal. Seek guidance from a qualified professional to strengthen your case through the appeals process.

Conclusion

The VA Aid and Attendance benefit provides essential financial assistance to surviving spouses of veterans. Understanding eligibility requirements, gathering complete documentation, and seeking help when needed are key to a successful application.
