Medicare and International Travel: What You Need to Know

Introduction

Traveling abroad is an exciting adventure, but for Medicare beneficiaries, understanding your health coverage while outside the United States is crucial. This guide provides a comprehensive overview of what Medicare does and does not cover during international travel, exceptions to the rules, and tips for protecting your health and your wallet while overseas.

Does Medicare Cover Healthcare Outside the United States?

In most cases, Original Medicare (Parts A and B) does not provide coverage for healthcare or medical services you receive outside the United States and its territories. This means that if you experience a medical emergency or require care while traveling internationally, you will likely have to pay out-of-pocket for those services.

Exceptions to Medicareâ€™s International Coverage Rules

Emergency Care in Canada While Traveling Between Alaska and Another State

If you are traveling directly between Alaska and another state and experience a medical emergency in Canada, Medicare may cover your care if a Canadian hospital is closer than the nearest U.S. hospital that can treat your emergency.

Medical Care on a Cruise Ship

Medicare may pay for medically necessary services on a cruise ship only if the ship is in U.S. territorial waters (within six hours of a U.S. port). If you are more than six hours away from a U.S. port, Medicare will not cover medical services, even in an emergency.

Care Received in a Foreign Hospital for a Medical Emergency Near the U.S. Border

Medicare may pay for emergency care in a foreign hospital if you are in the U.S. but the foreign hospital is closer than the nearest U.S. hospital that can treat your condition.

Hospital Care During a Medical Emergency While Traveling Through the U.S.

If you are traveling through the U.S. and experience a medical emergency that requires you to be admitted to a hospital, Medicare will cover your care as it would at home.

Medicare Supplement Plans and International Travel Coverage

Some Medicare Supplement Insurance (Medigap) plans offer additional international emergency health coverage. Plans C, D, F, G, M, and N cover 80% of the billed charges for certain medically necessary emergency care outside the U.S. after you meet a $250 deductible per year. Coverage is limited to the first 60 days of a trip and has a lifetime maximum of $50,000.

Medicare Plans That Cover Foreign Travel Emergencies

Some Medicare Advantage (Part C) plans may include coverage for international emergencies. Benefits and coverage vary by plan, so check with your provider about specifics before you travel.

Medigap Plan Availability

Not all Medigap plans are available in every state, and you must already be enrolled in Medicare Parts A and B to purchase a Medigap policy. If you travel frequently, consider choosing a Medigap plan with travel emergency benefits.

Medicare Advantage Plans and International Travel

Some Medicare Advantage plans offer worldwide emergency and urgent care coverage. Again, coverage and costs can vary significantly, so review your plan documents or contact your insurance provider before traveling.

Global Network Plans

A few insurance companies offer global network plans specifically for retirees who live overseas or travel extensively. These plans often provide broader international coverage than traditional Medicare or Medigap.

Supplemental Travel Insurance for Seniors

Because Medicareâ€™s international coverage is so limited, itâ€™s wise for seniors to purchase supplemental travel insurance before leaving the country. Travel insurance can help pay for medical emergencies, hospitalizations, evacuations, and even the cost of returning to the U.S. for treatment.

Types of Travel Insurance Coverage

Most travel insurance policies offer a range of options, including:

  • Emergency medical and dental coverage
  • Emergency medical evacuation
  • Trip cancellation or interruption
  • Lost luggage reimbursement

Carefully review the policy details to ensure it meets your health needs and travel plans.

Travel Insurance Providers for Seniors

Many reputable companies offer travel insurance for seniors, including:

  • Allianz Global Assistance
  • Travel Guard
  • AXA Assistance USA
  • Seven Corners

Preparing for International Travel: Key Considerations for Medicare Beneficiaries

Carry Important Medical Documents

Bring a list of your current medications, allergies, and medical conditions, as well as your Medicare card, Medigap or Medicare Advantage card, and travel insurance information.

Check for Travel Health Advisories and Vaccines

Visit the CDC and U.S. Department of State websites for the latest travel health alerts and required vaccinations for your destination.

Plan for Prescription Medications

Bring enough prescription medication to last your entire trip, plus a few extra days in case of delays. Carry medications in their original containers and bring copies of your prescriptions.

What to Do If You Need Medical Care Abroad

Contact Your Travel Insurance Provider

If you experience a medical emergency abroad, contact your travel insurance provider as soon as possible for guidance on where to get care and how to file a claim.

Pay for Care Out of Pocket and Seek Reimbursement

In most cases, you will need to pay for medical services upfront and then file a claim with your travel insurance or Medigap plan for reimbursement.

Request Medical Records

Be sure to request copies of your medical records, bills, and receipts before leaving the healthcare facility. These documents are essential for insurance claims and for sharing with your U.S. doctors.

Conclusion

Original Medicare offers very limited coverage outside the United States, so planning ahead is essential for safe and healthy international travel. Explore Medigap or Medicare Advantage plans with international emergency benefits, and always consider supplemental travel insurance for peace of mind. Know your plan, carry the right documents, and enjoy your travels with confidence!
